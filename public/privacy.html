Privacy Policy — World Time

Last updated: 2026-07-09

World Time (the website at defsix.github.io/time, and the Android and iOS apps that wrap it) does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal data. There is no account system, no analytics, no advertising SDK, and no server component of any kind — the app is a static site plus a thin native shell around it.

Location

If you grant location permission, your device's coordinates are used once, on launch, entirely on-device, to fly the globe to your nearest known city and show its local time. Your location is never transmitted anywhere, logged, or stored — the app doesn't have anywhere to send it to.

Alarms & notifications

If you set a city alarm (Android and iOS apps only), the alarm's time and label are stored locally on your device (Android SharedPreferences / iOS UserDefaults) purely so the app can list and cancel it later, and to reschedule it after a reboot. This data never leaves your device.

Time synchronization

The displayed time is cross-checked against a handful of independent public time APIs (see the Time Sources panel in the app for the exact list and live request details). These are plain unauthenticated HTTP requests for the current time — no identifying information is sent beyond what any standard web request includes (e.g. your IP address, visible to those third-party services the same way it would be to any website you visit).

Third-party services

Aside from the time-sync requests above, the app makes no other network requests and includes no third-party SDKs, trackers, or embedded content.

Children's privacy

The app collects no personal data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the update will be reflected here with a new "Last updated" date.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be raised via GitHub Issues on the project repository.
