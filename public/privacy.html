Privacy Policy — World Time

Last updated: 2026-07-09

Summary: no logs, no storage, no accounts, no analytics, no tracking, no ads, nothing sold or shared. World Time (the website at defsix.github.io/time, and the Android and iOS apps that wrap it) has no server component of any kind — it's a static site plus a thin native shell around it — so there's nowhere for your data to be logged even if the app wanted to. The only data ever kept is the bare minimum a feature needs to work, kept only on your own device, described exactly below.

Why location is requested

The app asks for location permission for exactly one reason: to find your nearest known city and fly the globe there on launch, so you see a relevant time zone immediately instead of a blank globe. That's the whole reason — there's no other feature that uses it.

Your coordinates are read once per launch, used entirely on-device to pick the nearest city from a bundled list, and then discarded. Nothing about your location is written to disk, logged, or transmitted anywhere — again, there's no server to send it to. Denying the permission just means the app falls back to your browser/device's time zone setting instead.

Alarms & notifications

If you set a city alarm (Android and iOS apps only), the alarm's time and a label you chose (e.g. a city name) are kept locally on your device (Android SharedPreferences / iOS UserDefaults) — the bare minimum needed so the app can list, cancel, and reschedule it after a reboot. Nothing about an alarm is logged or leaves your device.

Time synchronization

The displayed time is cross-checked against a handful of independent public time APIs (see the Time Sources panel in the app for the exact list and live request details). These are plain unauthenticated HTTP requests for the current time — no identifying information is sent beyond what any standard web request includes (e.g. your IP address, visible to those third-party services the same way it would be to any website you visit).

Third-party services

Aside from the time-sync requests above, the app makes no other network requests and includes no third-party SDKs, trackers, or embedded content.

Children's privacy

The app collects no personal data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the update will be reflected here with a new "Last updated" date.
